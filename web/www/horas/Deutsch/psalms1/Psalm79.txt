# Latin text of Psalm 80

# Line 79:2: "You who rule Israel, listen...", addressing God as the shepherd of Joseph
79:2 Qui regis Israël, inténde: * qui dedúcis velut ovem Joseph.

# Line 79:2: "You who sit enthroned upon the cherubim...", referring to God's presence in the Temple
79:2 Qui sedes super Chérubim, * manifestáre coram Éphraim, Bénjamin, et Manásse.

# Line 79:3: "Stir up your might...", asking God to intervene and save them
79:3 Éxcita poténtiam tuam, et veni, * ut salvos fácias nos.

# Line 79:4: "God, turn us back...", asking God to show them mercy and save them
79:4 Deus, convérte nos: * et osténde fáciem tuam, et salvi érimus.

# Line 79:5: "Lord, God of hosts, how long...", questioning why God is angry with their prayers
79:5 Dómine, Deus virtútum, * quoúsque irascéris super oratiónem servi tui?

# Line 79:6: "You feed us with the bread of tears...", describing their suffering and hardship
79:6 Cibábis nos pane lacrimárum: * et potum dabis nobis in lácrimis in mensúra?

# Line 79:7: "You have made us a reproach to our neighbors...", lamenting their humiliation and mockery
79:7 Posuísti nos in contradictiónem vicínis nostris: * et inimíci nostri subsannavérunt nos.

# Line 79:8: "God of hosts, turn us back...", repeating the plea for mercy and salvation
79:8 Deus virtútum, convérte nos: * et osténde fáciem tuam, et salvi érimus.

# Line 79:9: "You brought a vine out of Egypt...", referring to Israel's history and God's protection
79:9 Víneam de Ægýpto transtulísti: * ejecísti gentes, et plantásti eam.
