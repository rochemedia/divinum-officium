' [Lectio4] - Defines the fourth reading for the commemoration of the saint
' Gregory, to whom, is commonly given, on account of his extraordinary depth of sacred learning, the title of "the Divine" - Description of the saint
' was a noble Cappadocian, born at Nazianzus in that country, and educated at Athens along with St. Basil - Early life of the saint
' with whom likewise, when they had acquired knowledge in diverse branches of earthly learning, he gave himself up to learn the things of God - Turning to the sacred sciences
' They were both distinguished by power of doctrine and holiness of life, they were both called to the duty of preaching the Gospel of truth and, through the Gospel. they both begat many sons unto Christ - Achievements of the saint and St. Basil

' [Lectio5] - Defines the fifth reading for the commemoration of the saint
' Gregory after a while returned home - Returning to Nazianzus
' He was first made Bishop of Sasima, and afterwards administered the Church at Nazianzus - Ecclesiastical duties of the saint
' Then he was called to rule the Church of Constantinople - Ruling the Church of Constantinople
' That city, which he found reeking with heresy, he purged, and brought again to the Catholic faith - Purifying the Church of Constantinople
' But this, which deserved for him the warmest love of all men, raised up many enemies - Enemies of the saint
' Among the Bishops themselves there was a great party against him - Opposition from other bishops
' and to still their contentions, he, of his own free will, gave up his see - Resigning the See of Constantinople
' saying with the Prophet Jonah: "Take me up, and cast me forth into the sea so shall the sea be calm unto you for I know that for my sake this great tempest is upon you," (i. 12.) - Quoting the Prophet Jonah
' So he went his way back again to Nazianzus - Returning to Nazianzus
' and when he had seen that Eulalius was set over that Church, he gave himself up altogether to think and write concerning the things of God - Arranging for the Church of Nazianzus and returning to the sacred sciences
